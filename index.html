What is web 3? At 2001, Tim Barnes lee and some of his colleagues suggested Web 3.0 (semantic web) as the next stage after web 2.0. Web 3.0 will have the ability to allocate, analyze, phrase, display the data from the web in a better way more intuitive to the user. The user will have smarter and dynamic answers for his search, by using sensors around the web combined with new artificial intelligence algorithms. For example: someone had an accident and he needs a treatment, he will want to get answers on how to treat the injury on one hand but also how to sue the insurance, how long is for recovery, what are the most useful treatments he deserve to get from his insurance and other more complex questions. Todays "google" search is not supporting those kind of answers and the best they could get is "who is the current president of USA?" Is it the next trend? It has to be! The amount of data we consume and produce is increasing dramatically as more technologies arrive: smartphone, google glass, smart watches, and smart home (internet of things). Nevertheless the AI algorithms are more available and in use by developers than ever. Therefore, the revolution is already here and hopefully we could ask complicated questions and get good and reliable answers.

Web 3.0/semantic website: Thought there is not a full end-to-end solution to the example I stated, there are many sites that use semantic web protocols and make good use of data with Ai to facilitate the user life.
1) Ranker - crowd source ranking, uses semantic data from: freebase and factual
2) Tripit – an app that manage your trip, by lending then your booking orders, mails they use semantic data to make you a trip plan and advice you with flights weather concerns, coupons and more.
3) Evi - "evi can answer questions about local knowledge, weather, books, music, films, people and places, recipe ideas, shopping and much more." Uses AI algoithems and builds upon the core semantic search technology.
